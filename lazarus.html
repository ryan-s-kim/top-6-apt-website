Lazarus Group

North Korea-linked group associated with destructive attacks and financial theft.

Overview

Lazarus Group is a collective name used for a cluster of activity publicly linked to North Korea. It has been associated with espionage, disruptive operations, and large-scale theft from banks and cryptocurrency platforms.

Targets and objectives

The group has targeted financial institutions, cryptocurrency exchanges, governments, and critical infrastructure. Campaigns often combine revenue generation with strategic intelligence collection.

  • Banks and SWIFT-connected organizations.
  • Cryptocurrency exchanges and fintech platforms.
  • Government agencies and critical infrastructure networks.

Tactics and techniques

Lazarus Group has used spearphishing, watering-hole sites, supply-chain compromises, and custom malware families. Some operations deployed destructive malware and wiper components in addition to data theft.

  • Phishing emails and malicious documents.
  • Malware for remote access, credential theft, and lateral movement.
  • Destructive tooling in select campaigns.

Notable activity

Publicly discussed incidents linked to Lazarus Group include major financial heists and disruptive attacks on high-profile organizations.[web:14][web:32] These operations highlight the group's blend of state-linked and financially motivated behavior.[web:3][web:14]

Defensive tips

Recommended defenses include strict controls on payment systems, monitoring of outbound transfers, and strong segmentation between critical financial systems and user environments. Threat intelligence feeds focused on Lazarus indicators and behaviors can help organizations detect related activity earlier.
