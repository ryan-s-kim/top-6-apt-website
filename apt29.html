APT29 (Cozy Bear)

Russia-linked espionage group focused on governments and policy targets.

Overview

APT29, also called Cozy Bear and Midnight Blizzard, is associated in public reporting with Russian intelligence services. It is known for long-term, low-noise intrusions that aim to collect sensitive diplomatic and governmental information.

Targets and objectives

The group typically focuses on foreign ministries, embassies, research institutes, and policy organizations. Operations are usually geared toward strategic intelligence rather than quick financial gain.

  • Government and diplomatic networks.
  • Think tanks and policy research groups.
  • Technology and cloud service providers supporting high-value organizations.

Tactics and techniques

Analysts often map APT29 behavior to ATT&CK techniques like spearphishing, credential theft, and living off the land using built-in tools. In recent campaigns, the group has heavily abused cloud identities, OAuth applications, and legitimate remote access methods.

  • Spearphishing emails and malicious links to capture credentials.
  • Use of legitimate admin tools and PowerShell to blend into normal activity.
  • Targeting cloud email and collaboration platforms for persistent access.

Notable activity

Public reports link APT29 to multiple campaigns against Western governments and international organizations, including compromises of email systems and identity infrastructure. Some intrusions used supply-chain-style access by abusing service providers to reach many downstream entities.

Defensive tips

Defenders often focus on hardening identity systems, enforcing multifactor authentication, and closely monitoring sign-in patterns to detect APT29-style activity. Mapping local controls to ATT&CK techniques associated with APT29 helps identify detection and logging gaps.
