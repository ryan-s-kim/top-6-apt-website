Cyber Threat Intelligence & APTs

Understanding advanced persistent threats and how they target organizations.

What is CTI?

Cyber threat intelligence (CTI) is analyzed information about threats, adversaries, and their techniques that helps defenders make better security decisions.

Why APTs matter

Advanced persistent threats (APTs) are skilled, well-resourced groups that conduct long-term intrusions against high-value targets like governments and critical infrastructure.

How CTI is used

Security teams use CTI to map attacker techniques, prioritize defenses, and quickly detect suspicious activity across their networks.

Types of threat intelligence

High-level overview

Threat intelligence is often grouped into strategic, operational, tactical, and technical levels, each aimed at different audiences and decisions.

Using frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK

Practical defender tools

The MITRE ATT&CK framework maps attacker behaviors to specific techniques so defenders can test coverage, detect gaps, and build better detections.
