APT28 (Fancy Bear)

Russia-linked group known for operations against military, political, and media targets.

Overview

APT28, often called Fancy Bear, is a threat group widely reported as aligned with Russian military intelligence. It has been linked to high-profile intrusions and influence-related operations targeting NATO members and political organizations.

Targets and objectives

The group frequently focuses on defense, foreign affairs, media, and political entities in Europe and North America. Activity often aims to collect sensitive information and sometimes support information operations.

  • Military and defense organizations.
  • Political parties, campaigns, and NGOs.
  • Media outlets and policy think tanks.

Tactics and techniques

Reporting describes APT28 as using spearphishing, credential harvesting, custom malware, and exploitation of vulnerabilities in public-facing services. The group has also used malware families tailored for long-term access and data theft.

  • Spearphishing emails with malicious attachments or links.
  • Exploiting unpatched software to gain initial access.
  • Use of backdoors and tunneling tools for persistence and exfiltration.

Notable activity

APT28 has been publicly tied to compromises of political organizations and campaigns, as well as long-running operations against defense-related targets. Some activity has overlapped with broader efforts to influence public opinion and strategic narratives.

Defensive tips

Strong patch management, phishing-resistant authentication, and monitoring of high-value accounts are common recommendations against APT28-style threats. Security teams often map detections to ATT&CK techniques used by APT28 to validate coverage.
