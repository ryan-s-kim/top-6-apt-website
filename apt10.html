APT10 (Stone Panda)

China-linked actor focused on intellectual property theft and managed service providers.

Overview

APT10, sometimes referred to as Stone Panda, is associated with long-running espionage campaigns targeting organizations worldwide. It is especially known for compromising managed service providers (MSPs) to gain access to many downstream customers.

Targets and objectives

The group has targeted technology, manufacturing, healthcare, and other sectors to obtain intellectual property and sensitive business information. By focusing on MSPs, APT10 can reach a large number of client networks through a single compromise.

  • Managed service providers and cloud service operators.
  • High-tech and manufacturing companies.
  • Healthcare and other data-rich industries.

Tactics and techniques

APT10 has used spearphishing, credential theft, and custom malware families deployed through MSP infrastructure. Activity often emphasizes persistence and quiet data exfiltration over long periods.

  • Phishing emails and remote access trojans.
  • Compromise of MSP management tools and remote access channels.
  • Use of staged exfiltration to avoid detection.

Notable activity

Public reporting describes global campaigns where APT10 accessed multiple customer environments through compromised MSP networks. These intrusions often focused on sensitive intellectual property and strategic corporate data.

Defensive tips

Organizations are advised to review MSP access, enforce least privilege, and monitor MSP-originated connections closely. Strong logging and anomaly detection on remote management channels can help reveal APT10-style activity.
